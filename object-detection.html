01 — Overview

Background & Purpose

A traffic accident is any case that creates a traffic hazard. The social cost of traffic accidents is estimated at $32 billion USD a year in South Korea. According to data from the Seoul Metropolitan Police Agency, Gangseo-gu is among the districts with the most frequent traffic fatalities in Seoul (2020). According to 2017 Traffic Statistics, fatalities on living roads reached 57% of all traffic fatality cases.

To make Gangseo-gu safer, this project analyzed the causes of traffic accidents from 2016 to 2020 and proposed a data-backed solution to reduce accidents at alleyway intersections.

Project Goal

When a vehicle approaches an alleyway intersection where visibility is not secured, the existing CCTV camera on the road will detect moving objects in real time and activate a warning light to alert drivers and pedestrians coming from the crossing direction — before a collision occurs.

Hwagok 1-dong Deep Dive

Figure 5. Hwagok 1-dong alleyway accident locations — red: car-to-person, white: car-to-car

Hwagok 1-dong is predominantly composed of living roads. Accidents cluster heavily at three-way and four-way alleyway intersections (red-circled in Figure 5) — confirming that intersection blind spots are the core driver of accident frequency.

Key Finding

Most alley accidents are caused by collisions with people or vehicles from crossing directions due to near-zero visibility. Cars parked on both sides further reduce visibility and aggravate the problem. The solution: use existing CCTV infrastructure to detect approaching objects and trigger warning lights at the highest-risk intersections — before a collision, not after.

04 — OpenCV MOG2

Object tracking algorithm

Among real-time background removal methods, the Gaussian Mixture Model (MOG2) is effective and widely used. MOG2 handles slow lighting changes, periodic background movement, slow-moving objects, long-term scene changes, and camera noise — all common in outdoor CCTV environments.

MOG2 compares each incoming frame against previous frames, removes the static background (set to black), and extracts moving objects (expressed in white). Morphological operations then clean and highlight the detected objects to produce reliable outlines. When an outline crosses the warning red line, the warning system activates.

Morphological Techniques

To accurately track objects, noise must be removed while the target object is preserved and expanded. Three techniques are applied in sequence:

Figure 7. Erosion and Dilation — the building blocks of morphological processing
Figure 8. Closing — fills holes without amplifying noise
Figure 9. Opening — removes noise while preserving object shape
01

Closing Technique

Dilation followed by erosion — expands the object first to fill internal holes, then erodes back to original size without increasing surrounding noise. Green object is emphasized; red noise stays minimal.

02

Opening Technique

Erosion followed by dilation — removes surrounding noise while minimizing loss of the object itself. Object shape is preserved; red noise is effectively eliminated.

03

Dilation × 2

Applied twice after closing and opening to expand distant objects — without this, small far-away objects fail to produce a visible outline and go undetected.

Parameter Tuning

MOG2's varThreshold was increased from default 16 to 80 — reducing false detections from reflectors and road markings at the cost of slightly lower sensitivity. Detection zone was restricted to road areas only. The final model is significantly more stable than the early prototype, with greatly improved real-world recognition accuracy.

05 — Outcome

Result & impact

🏆

Big Data Competition Winner

The project won 3rd place in a Big Data competition for combining GIS spatial analysis with real-time object detection to address a genuine public safety problem — and delivering a technically feasible, data-backed solution.

Utilization Plan & Expected Effect

The system applies to any alley where vehicles, motorcycles, bicycles, and pedestrians share space. Highest impact is expected at three-way and four-way alley intersections where driver visibility is near zero.

By leveraging existing CCTV infrastructure and adding warning lights at intersections, the system warns both directions simultaneously when an approaching object is detected within a threshold distance — stopping potential collisions before they happen. This is expected to significantly reduce Gangseo-gu's alley accident rate without major new infrastructure investment.
